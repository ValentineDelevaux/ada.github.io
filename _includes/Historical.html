Hall of historical events

Ah, welcome, dear guest, to this grand tour of Dracula's most cherished abode. As you may know, my master, Dracula, has dedicated countless years—centuries, in fact—to the study of human fears and the evolution of horror films. His thirst for knowledge is matched only by his thirst for blood. But let us not digress. Allow me to guide you through a few key moments in history that have most profoundly shaped the world of horror cinema. Master Dracula, with his unparalleled longevity, has observed all these events with great interest.

Allow me to begin with a rather unsettling topic: pandemic. As you see on the graph here, there is one year that stands out: the swine flu pandemic in 2009. It was the third major flu pandemic tied to the H1N1 virus, affecting up to 11% to 21% of the global population. The outbreak’s sheer scale—a number Dracula himself finds quite respectable—was indeed significant, with its p-value at 5%. Similarly, the Russian flu pandemic of 1977, which primarily afflicted individuals under 25, also shows a significant impact at 10%." "My master was quite surprised, however, that the recent COVID-19 pandemic of 2020 did not yield higher significance in this study. After all, it is the second deadliest pandemic since 1960! But when Dracula dug deeper into the data—oh, how he enjoys his studies—he found that with a lower statistical threshold, such events become far more prominent. As for the deadliest pandemic, the HIV/AIDS pandemic, Dracula notes its absence from this plot. Its continuous spread over decades likely created subtle but recurring noise in the data. Even now, my master marvels at the persistent fear such unseen enemies provoke.

Now, moving on, let us discuss something else that might send a chill down your spine: the petrol crises.

Here, we observe how major petrol crises have rippled through the horror genre. The Gulf War in 1990 stands out as the most significant event, with its p-value at 5%. Dracula would remind you that this conflict saw Iraq invade Kuwait, sparking an oil supply disruption and skyrocketing prices, events that bled into every corner of life, even entertainment. At that time, the horror genre reflected this global unease, much to Dracula’s interest. The oil embargo of 1973, orchestrated by OAPEC during the Yom Kippur War, caused barrel prices to triple, an economic shock so profound it influenced the very words filmmakers used in their horror summaries. The COVID 19 pandemic plummeted the demand for oil as lockdowns, travel restrictions, and business closures reduced economic activity worldwide, explaining the significance f this event. My master would also have you note the global financial crisis of 2008, which, combined with the petrol price crash in December of that year, resulted in the only event between 1995 and 2020 during which the number of horror movies declined, being significant at 10%. Dracula attributes this to reduced consumer spending and shifts in audience behavior, a clear reminder that even he is not immune to the whims of mortal economies. Oh, the ironies!

Now, let us move on to something even more unnerving: the topic of communism.

The fall of the Berlin Wall in 1989 marks the apex of this fear, showing the highest peak on our graph two years later, in 1991. Dracula has noted that this aligns with the minimum time required to produce a film after such an event. The data also shows a curious peak in 1995, which may reflect the lingering influence of the Soviet Union’s dissolution in 1991. My master was quite impressed by how fear of communism once dominated the minds of filmmakers, weaving its way into the lexical fields of horror cinema." Since that zenith in 1991, the influence of communism on horror has slowly faded, much like the shadow of an ancient foe vanquished by time. Dracula, ever the astute observer, sees this as evidence of capitalism’s growing dominance in shaping mortal fears. Ah, how fleeting the terrors of men are compared to the centuries of fear inspired by my master!

I hope these insights into history's influence on horror cinema have intrigued you, my dear guest. As for Dracula, he remains ever passionate in his study of fear, though I must warn you, his moods can be... unsettling at times. Now, if you'll follow me, we shall continue the tour. And should you wish to delve deeper, Dracula’s library is always open.
